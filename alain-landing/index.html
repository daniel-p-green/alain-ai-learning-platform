The gap between releases and learning

  • Model releases outpace learning
  • Adoption concentrates on a few providers; the long tail stalls
  • Retries and delays rise as docs fall behind

Today, models ship faster than teams can learn. Docs become guesses, retries, and delays. Adoption clusters around a few providers, while the long tail, often cheaper or better for specific jobs, gets ignored.

An instruction booklet for every model

  • Open‑source blueprints that teach by doing
  • Works across providers; pick the best fit for each job
  • Reusable lessons with guardrails, citations, and quick checks

Alain is the open‑source blueprint layer for all models. Think IKEA for AI. Models are the raw materials. Alain is the instruction booklet that turns them into things you use every day.

Data and Privacy

Do lessons send my data to third parties?
No. Use the local‑first path via Ollama to run everything on your machine.
How are citations enforced?
Required citations are embedded into notebooks and linted before sharing.
Can my team review guardrails?
Yes. Guardrails are visible, versioned, and editable in each notebook.
What about credentials and secrets?
Use local environment variables or vault providers; secrets never appear in notebooks.
